🔑 Diagnostic Requirements

  • Symptoms present ≥ 2 weeks
  • Most of the day, nearly every day
  • Significant distress OR functional impairment
  • Not caused by substances or medical condition
  • Must include depressed mood OR anhedonia

If a classmate had never heard of depression, how would you explain what makes it a "disorder" — not just sadness — in 3 sentences?
Depression is more than sadness — it is a biological illness where specific neurotransmitters are depleted, causing changes that persist for ≥ 2 weeks, most of the day. Unlike normal grief or a bad day, MDD impairs function — work, relationships, and self-care all suffer. It also carries high suicide risk, making it a true medical emergency requiring treatment, not willpower.
🔬 Chapter Two
The Monoamine
Hypothesis
Depression arises from insufficient neurotransmitter activity. Every drug class targets one or more deficits.
🧠 Core ConceptDepression = ↓ Serotonin + ↓ Norepinephrine + ↓ Dopamine. Every antidepressant class targets one or more of these deficits.

🔴 Serotonin (5-HT)

  • Regulates mood, sleep, appetite
  • ↓ = depression + anxiety
  • SSRIs specifically target this
  • GI motility → explains GI side effects

🔵 Norepinephrine (NE)

  • Regulates energy, alertness, focus
  • ↓ = fatigue, poor concentration
  • Targeted by SNRIs and TCAs
  • Also modulates pain pathways

🟡 Dopamine (DA)

  • Regulates motivation, pleasure, reward
  • ↓ = anhedonia (key symptom)
  • Primarily targeted by bupropion
  • Role in psychomotor activity

Which NT is specifically targeted by SSRIs?
Serotonin (5-HT) — SSRIs block its reuptake pump → more serotonin in synapse
Which drug targets DA + NE but NOT serotonin? Why does that matter?
Bupropion — no serotonin = no sexual dysfunction side effects
Which class inhibits the enzyme breaking down ALL monoamines?
MAOIs — inhibit monoamine oxidase → ↑ serotonin, NE, AND dopamine simultaneously

Clinical Features
of Depression
🚨 Always Assess FirstSuicidal ideation or behavior = #1 priority. Safety before all other assessment or intervention.

💔 Emotional

  • Depressed mood (core symptom)
  • Anhedonia — loss of interest
  • Hopelessness, helplessness
  • Worthlessness, excessive guilt
  • Irritability (especially teens)

🧠 Cognitive

  • Poor concentration, indecisiveness
  • Negative distorted thinking
  • Memory impairment
  • Suicidal ideation or plan

🛌 Physical (Neurovegetative)

  • Sleep: insomnia OR hypersomnia
  • Appetite: anorexia OR hyperphagia
  • Psychomotor agitation or retardation
  • Fatigue, loss of energy

⚠️ Critical Safety Window — Weeks 1–3Energy returns BEFORE mood lifts. Patient now has energy to act on suicidal thoughts. This is the HIGHEST-RISK period. Monitor most closely here. No improvement at 4 weeks → reassess.

🚨 Black Box Warning

  • Highest risk: patients under 25
  • Monitor mood and behavior changes
  • Watch for worsening depression or agitation
  • Prescribe small quantities — limit lethal means

⚡ ECT Key Facts

  • Fastest-acting treatment for depression
  • Performed under general anesthesia
  • Does NOT permanently damage brain tissue
  • Typical course: 6–12 sessions
  • Also: TMS, vagus nerve stimulation, light therapy

Critical Safety Points
⭐ Sexual Dysfunction — #1 Reason Patients StopDecreased libido, delayed orgasm, erectile dysfunction. Educate BEFORE starting — most common reason for silent non-compliance.
⏳ Onset — Most Tested FactInitial: ~1–2 weeks (sleep/energy). Full mood benefit: 4–6 weeks. Black box: suicidal ideation in patients under 25.

🔥 Serotonin Syndrome 🔥 Critical

Onset: 2–72 hrs after drug addition/change
1. Mental status changes — agitation, confusion
2. Neuromuscular — hyperreflexia, clonus, tremor
3. Autonomic — hyperthermia, diaphoresis, tachycardia
ActionSTOP the drug · Supportive care · Cyproheptadine (antidote)
Which SSRI treats Bulimia AND has the longest half-life?
Fluoxetine (Prozac) — 5-week washout required before starting an MAOI
Name the 3-symptom triad of Serotonin Syndrome
1. Altered mental status 2. Neuromuscular excitability (hyperreflexia/clonus) 3. Autonomic instability (fever/diaphoresis)
Which SSRI has QT prolongation risk? What to monitor?
Citalopram (Celexa) — monitor ECG in cardiac patients; maximum dose restrictions apply

TCAs
Handle With Care
⚠️ High-Risk ClassRarely first-line due to lethal overdose potential and heavy side effect burden. Use only when SSRIs/SNRIs fail.

💊 Examples

  • Imipramine — prototype
  • Amitriptyline (Elavil)
  • Clomipramine (OCD — second line)
  • Nortriptyline

🔬 Why So Many Side Effects?

TCAs block NE + serotonin reuptake, but ALSO block:

  • Muscarinic receptors → anticholinergic effects
  • Alpha-1 adrenergic → orthostatic hypotension
  • Histamine receptors → sedation
Side Effects by System

🧠 Anticholinergic

  • Dry mouth
  • Constipation
  • Urinary retention
  • Blurred vision
  • Confusion (elderly!)

❤️ Cardiovascular

  • Orthostatic hypotension
  • Dysrhythmias
  • QT prolongation
  • Tachycardia

💊 CNS

  • Sedation (significant)
  • Confusion
  • Seizures at toxic levels
💀 TCA Overdose — Life-ThreateningTachycardia · Dysrhythmias · Ventricular fibrillation · Wide QRS (classic ECG sign) · Seizures
Treatment: Activated charcoal + Sodium bicarbonate · ICU monitoring
Nursing: Give 1-week supply ONLY. High lethality = limit lethal means in depressed patients.
A patient asks: "Why can't I get 3 months of TCAs refilled at once like my blood pressure medication?" How do you explain this?
"That's a really good question. Unlike blood pressure medications, this medication can cause serious heart rhythm problems if too much is taken at once. That makes a large supply risky — not because we don't trust you, but because depression can sometimes change a person's thinking in ways that put them at risk. Keeping the supply small is a safety measure we use for everyone on this medication."

"That's actually one of the most common reasons people switch. There's another antidepressant called bupropion (Wellbutrin) that works on completely different brain chemicals — dopamine and norepinephrine rather than serotonin. 🪤 Exam Prep
NCLEX Traps
& Pitfalls
❌
Trap #1 — "Medication works within days"WRONG for all antidepressants. Initial effects at 1–2 weeks (sleep, energy). Full mood benefit: 4–6 weeks. Correct teaching: "It may take 3–4 weeks to notice mood improvement."
❌
Trap #2 — MAOI + SSRI with "a few days" betweenDEADLY. Must wait 14 days after stopping most SSRIs, and 5 WEEKS after stopping fluoxetine. This combination causes fatal serotonin syndrome.
❌
Trap #3 — "Any fresh food is fine on a MAOI"WRONG. Even some "fresh" foods (overripe banana, avocado, fava beans) contain tyramine. Aged, fermented, cured, and smoked = always off limits.
❌
Trap #4 — Stopping antidepressants abruptly is fineWRONG. Discontinuation syndrome: flu-like symptoms, "brain zaps," dizziness, irritability. Always taper. Applies to SSRIs, SNRIs, and TCAs.
❌
Trap #5 — Bupropion safe in seizure/eating disorder historyWRONG. Absolute contraindication. Seizure disorder, head injury, anorexia, and bulimia all lower seizure threshold — combined with bupropion is dangerous.
❌
Trap #6 — Energy returning = less monitoring neededOPPOSITE. The early energy return before mood lifts is the HIGHEST risk window for suicide. Increase monitoring, not decrease.
❌
Trap #7 — Diphenhydramine (Benadryl) is safe with TCAsWRONG. Both highly anticholinergic → additive toxicity: urinary retention, confusion, delirium, blurred vision — dangerous especially in elderly.
❌
Trap #8 — "Serotonin syndrome is just feeling jittery"WRONG. It's a triad: altered mental status + neuromuscular excitability + autonomic instability. Can be FATAL. Stop the drug immediately.

Q1
Select All That Apply
A nurse is discharging a patient newly prescribed sertraline for MDD. Which teaching points should be included? Select all that apply.
A"Sleep and energy may improve in 1–2 weeks, but full mood benefit takes 4–6 weeks."
B"If you feel no different after 3 days, stop the medication — it isn't working."
C"Report any new or worsening thoughts of self-harm to your provider immediately."
D"Do not stop this medication abruptly — always consult your provider first."
E"Sexual side effects such as decreased libido are possible — let your provider know."
✅ Correct: A, C, D, E

A — Accurate onset timeline: sleep/energy 1–2 weeks, full mood benefit 4–6 weeks.

C — Black box warning — suicidal ideation risk especially under-25. Must report immediately.

D — Abrupt discontinuation = discontinuation syndrome. Always taper.

E — Sexual dysfunction is the most common reason patients stop SSRIs. Proactive education prevents silent non-compliance.

B — WRONG: Never stop after 3 days. SSRIs require weeks. Abrupt stops cause discontinuation syndrome.
Q2
Expected / Unexpected
A patient on phenelzine (Nardil) calls after a dinner party reporting a severe throbbing headache, stiff neck, and racing heart. The nurse MOST correctly identifies this as:
AAn expected MAOI side effect manageable with acetaminophen.
BAn UNEXPECTED, dangerous sign of hypertensive crisis from tyramine ingestion — emergency evaluation needed.
CExpected discontinuation syndrome symptoms.
DAn expected antidepressant initiation reaction that will resolve in 2 weeks.
✅ Correct: B

Classic tyramine reaction after a dinner party. Severe throbbing headache + stiff neck + cardiovascular symptoms = MAOI hypertensive crisis. This is a medical emergency. Treatment: phentolamine, nitroprusside, or labetalol. NOT a normal side effect.
Q3
Appropriate / Not Appropriate
A 21-year-old started fluoxetine 10 days ago. His mother calls: "He has more energy now and is up making plans, but still says everything is hopeless." Which nursing response is MOST appropriate?
A"The returning energy is great — the medication is working. Call back in 2 weeks."
B"This is the critical safety window. Energy returns before mood lifts, raising suicide risk. Bring him in today — do not leave him alone."
C"Since he still feels hopeless, stop the medication and try a different one."
D"The medication takes 4–6 weeks — have him rest more to reduce the energy surge."
✅ Correct: B

The energy-before-mood window is the most dangerous period of SSRI therapy. The patient has renewed energy but unresolved hopelessness — the highest-risk scenario for suicidal action. Same-day evaluation required. This is a safety emergency.

Q5
Appropriate Response
A patient with severe treatment-resistant depression is prescribed ECT. The daughter asks: "Why are they doing shock therapy — isn't that dangerous?" Which response is MOST appropriate?
A"ECT is experimental and has many dangerous long-term side effects."
B"ECT is safe and effective — it's the fastest-acting treatment for severe depression, done under anesthesia, with temporary memory changes as the main side effect."
C"ECT permanently damages brain tissue but is the only option left."
D"I can't discuss this — you'll need to speak with the doctor."
✅ Correct: B

ECT is legitimate, evidence-based, and fastest-acting for severe/refractory depression. Performed under anesthesia, does NOT permanently damage brain tissue, main side effect is temporary memory loss. Nurses can and should provide factual education to family members.
Q6
Select All That Apply

Q9
